The Scenario: Barnet FC – The Bees' Return

Barnet FC, affectionately known as "The Bees," boasts a rich history, having played at their iconic Underhill Stadium in the London Borough of Barnet for 106 years. In 2013, due to various constraints with Underhill, the club relocated to The Hive Stadium in the neighboring borough of Harrow. While The Hive has served as a functional home, the emotional and historical ties to Barnet remain strong amongst the fanbase and local community.

After a decade away, Barnet FC's leadership has announced an ambitious strategic initiative: to return to the London Borough of Barnet by developing a new, purpose-built stadium. This project, with an estimated budget of £25 million and a proposed 3-year timeline, is more than just a construction project; it's a symbol of homecoming, community regeneration, and a new chapter for the club, which currently competes in the National League (the fifth tier of English football).

The move is fraught with complexities: securing planning permissions, community engagement, financial viability, managing supporter expectations, and ensuring the new stadium meets modern standards while respecting the club's heritage. Success will depend on meticulous planning, robust analysis, and effective stakeholder management.

Project Snapshot:

  • Budget: £25 million
  • Timeline: 3 years
  • Current League: National League (5th tier)

Section 1: PESTLE Analysis (30 mins)

Objective: To identify and analyze the key external macro-environmental factors that could influence Barnet FC's stadium relocation project.

Your Task: In your teams, brainstorm and document factors under each PESTLE category. Identify at least 2-3 distinct factors per category, explaining their potential impact. Use your flip chart or digital whiteboard.

P - Political ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • How might local council elections or changes in political leadership impact planning permissions?
  • What national government policies on sports infrastructure or funding could affect the project?

Challenging Factor:

A newly elected local council has a manifesto commitment to prioritize social housing over large leisure developments.

E - Economic ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • How might the current economic climate (inflation, construction costs) impact the £25 million budget?
  • What are potential economic benefits for the local Barnet area?

Challenging Factor:

A major local employer announces significant layoffs, reducing disposable income.

S - Social ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • What is the current sentiment of the Barnet community towards the club's return?
  • How might demographic shifts influence stadium design/services?
  • Are there concerns about disruption, traffic, or noise?

Challenging Factor:

A vocal residents' group emerges, concerned about impact on local parkland.

T - Technological ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • What modern stadium technologies should be considered (cashless, Wi-Fi, sustainability)?
  • How can technology enhance fan engagement?

Challenging Factor:

The proposed site has poor existing digital infrastructure.

L - Legal ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • What are key planning permission requirements and potential legal hurdles?
  • Are there specific safety/accessibility regulations for new sports venues?

Challenging Factor:

An ancient right of way is discovered across a section of the proposed land.

E - Environmental ▼

Prompting Questions:

  • What are environmental considerations (green building, waste, wildlife)?
  • How can the club demonstrate commitment to sustainability?

Challenging Factor:

The proposed site is adjacent to a protected nature reserve.

Section 3: CATWOE Analysis (30 mins)

Objective: To analyze the perspectives and needs of your Top 3 critical stakeholders, providing deeper understanding for requirements elicitation.

Your Task: For EACH of your Top 3 stakeholders identified in the previous section, conduct a CATWOE analysis using your template/whiteboard.

The CATWOE Framework:

  • C (Customers/Clients): Who benefits or suffers from the transformation (from this stakeholder's POV)?
  • A (Actors): Who carries out the activities in the transformation (from this stakeholder's POV)?
  • T (Transformation Process): What's the core change? (Input -> Output, e.g., undeveloped land -> community asset).
  • W (Weltanschauung/Worldview): What's the stakeholder's core belief making this meaningful? (e.g., "Barnet FC belongs in Barnet").
  • O (Owners): Who has ultimate authority to approve/stop it (from this stakeholder's POV)?
  • E (Environmental Constraints): What external factors impact it? (Link to PESTLE).

Example Questions (for David Miller, Barnet FC CEO):

  • C: Who ultimately benefits if this relocation is a success from his perspective? Who suffers if it fails?
  • W: What's his core belief driving this? ("A new stadium is essential for the club's long-term survival and growth").
